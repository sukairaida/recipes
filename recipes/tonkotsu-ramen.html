Tonkotsu Ramen

Description

Tonkotsu ramen is a rich and creamy pork bone broth ramen that originated in Fukuoka, Japan. It's known for its deeply savory flavor and silky texture.

Ingredients

  • 2 lbs pork bones
  • 8 cups water
  • 1 inch ginger, sliced
  • 4 cloves garlic
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 2 tablespoons miso paste
  • Ramen noodles
  • Soft boiled eggs
  • Green onions
  • Nori sheets

Steps

  1. Boil the pork bones in water for 10 minutes, then rinse and clean.
  2. Return bones to a pot, add fresh water, ginger, and garlic. Simmer for 12 hours.
  3. Strain the broth, then mix in soy sauce and miso paste.
  4. Cook ramen noodles according to package instructions.
  5. Serve noodles in broth and top with eggs, green onions, and nori.
